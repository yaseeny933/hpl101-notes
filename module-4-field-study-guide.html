00 The assignment, in one box

The Problem of Practice (PoP) asks you to choose one field study (One Village, One Preschool or Road to Success Academy) and respond at one level of practice (Learner, Program, or System). Each prompt poses a decision between two options; you make and defend an argument, citing course concepts from the module (you may draw on earlier modules too).

The three levels = whose vantage point you argue from: Learner = frontline, working directly with learners (a teacher / staff member) · Program = running the organization (director / manager, partnerships & staff development) · System = the wider policy / governance layer (government official / school board).

01 Field Study A — One Village, One Preschool (OVOP)

OVOP · rural China

What it is: An initiative expanding access to early-childhood education in remote rural China through Village Early Education Centers (VEECs), run with the China Development Research Foundation (CDRF) and, increasingly, local government. It serves young children — many from migrant-worker families and the Miao and other ethnic-minority groups — aiming to foster relationships, social-emotional development, early literacy, and culturally/linguistically responsive learning.

Learner Lead VEEC teacher

You have curricular autonomy. Students feel disconnected from school and the academic material. Two promising fixes — and limited prep time means you pick one to prioritize.

Decide: new social-emotional learning lessons  vs.  new curricular materials integrating students' cultural & linguistic identities.

  • Relationships & care; trustworthy teachers — belonging drives engagement 4.1.1 · 4.1.2 · 4.4.1
  • Culturally responsive / relevant / sustaining pedagogy — using Miao language & culture as an asset 4.3.3
  • Attributed vs. internalized learner identity; making school feel "for me" 4.3.1 · 4.3.2
  • Funds of knowledge / asset (not deficit) framing 3.2.1 · 3.2.4
  • Authentic learning & motivation; ZPD / scaffolding for connection to material 2.3.3 · 1.4.2

02 Field Study B — Road to Success Academy (RTSA)

RTSA · LA County juvenile justice

What it is: An education approach for youth in U.S. juvenile-justice facilities (Los Angeles County camps), developed with guidance from HGSE's Dr. Lynette Tannis. It emphasizes healing, relationships, and social-emotional learning to prepare young people for fulfilling lives beyond the carceral system — a counter to the school-to-prison pipeline. (Content touches the dehumanization and structural violence of youth incarceration.)

Learner Staff professional at RTSA

New students struggle to adjust and feel part of the community; relationships are among the biggest factors in success. A new initiative helps each student build one close relationship on arrival.

Decide: focus that relationship on a peer who has adjusted well  vs.  a staff member / adult.

  • Building relationships through care; trusting & trustworthy adults 4.4.1 · 4.1.2
  • Bonding vs. bridging skills — peer vs. adult tie 4.4.3
  • Peer relationships & their role in learning 4.1.3
  • Belonging / psychological safety as a condition for learning 4.1.1 · 4.1.4
  • SEL competencies; buffering against toxic stress in a high-threat setting 4.4.2 · 1.3.1 · 1.3.2
  • Attributed & internalized learner identity; countering stigma 4.3.1 · 4.3.2

Program RTSA program manager

You're planning a quarterly PD "Saturday Summit" on one aspect of SEL: supporting students' identity development. Limited time means one focus.

Decide: prioritize identity as learners  vs.  ethnic-racial identity (ERI) development.

★ Building your argument

  • Name the tension. Each prompt is a genuine trade-off — say what each option gains and costs before you choose.
  • Lead with 2–3 theories, not ten. Pick the concepts that most directly justify your choice; define each in your own words and tie it to the specific scenario.
  • Match the level. A Learner-level answer talks about this teacher and these students; a System-level answer talks about policy, equity, and scale. Use evidence appropriate to the vantage point.
  • Acknowledge the other option. The strongest arguments concede the merits of the path you didn't choose, then explain why your choice wins for this context.
  • Cite the module. Reference Module 4 concepts by name (relationships, identity, SEL, culturally sustaining pedagogy) — and reach back to earlier modules where it strengthens the case.
